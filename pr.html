Пріоритети: a tool that argues with you about what matters

April 17, 2026 — Kyiv, Ukraine

Most priority systems give you one answer. Пріоритети gives you three, and they disagree.

Пріоритети is a personal decision-training tool that runs three independent prioritization frameworks against the same task list. When they agree, you get a clear signal. When they don't, the disagreement itself is the insight: it surfaces which voice you're actually listening to.

The problem

Productivity tools tell you what to do next. They optimize for throughput. But the hard problem isn't knowing what's urgent. It's knowing whether urgency is the right lens.

A deadline screams. Personal growth whispers. Your energy level on a given morning changes everything. No single framework captures all three forces at once, and when they conflict, that conflict is where the real decision lives.

Three frameworks, one decision

Consequences x Deadline
What breaks if you don't do this? Score: 1-9. Irreversible consequences with a today deadline score highest.
Personal Importance x Impact Scope
Does this align with who you want to be? Score: 1-9. Life-changing decisions affecting many areas score highest.
Energy Match
Can you do this well right now? Score: 2-4. Perfect match between required and available energy scores highest.

When two or more frameworks agree on a top task, a consensus banner appears. Lock it as today's focus. When all three point at different tasks, a conflict state shows each framework's pick. You choose which voice to follow.

Daily ritual, not a task manager

Пріоритети is not a to-do list. It's a twice-daily ritual.

  • Morning: set your energy level, review framework rankings, commit to one task for the day. If yesterday had a locked task, the morning retrospective asks: was that the right choice?
  • Evening: close the day with a reflection. The app records whether you followed the frameworks' consensus or chose your own path. Alignment tracked over time builds a decision streak.

The goal isn't to always follow the frameworks. It's to notice when you override them and why.

Design

Brutalist. Typography-driven. No gradients, no shadows, no rounded corners, no emoji. Light theme on warm paper, like a government document or a surgeon's checklist.

Typography is Brygada 1918, a Ukrainian-Polish historical serif designed in 1918, paired with Arsenal, a Ukrainian-designed sans-serif. The interface is in Ukrainian. OKLCH color palette with warm neutrals and a single red accent for urgency.

The content is the interface. No decoration that doesn't carry information.

Technical

  • Single-file React app, no build step, runs in the browser
  • Vercel serverless API with Upstash Redis for cross-device sync
  • PWA installable on phone home screen
  • Ukrainian/English language toggle, data stays in Ukrainian internally
  • localStorage cache for offline-first loading
  • 38 automated tests covering scoring engine and UI components
