## Design Context

### Users
Single user. Ukrainian-speaking professional who misjudges urgency between work and personal priorities. Opens the app twice daily: morning ritual (set energy, see rankings, commit to one task) and evening close (reflection, streak). Primarily on phone, sometimes desktop.

### Brand Personality
Sovereign. Unvarnished. Deliberate. This is a thinking tool, not a productivity app. It should feel like having a conversation with your own judgment, not like using software.

### Aesthetic Direction
Brutalist / raw. Light theme. No decoration, no gradients, no polish. Like a government document, a surgeon's checklist, handwritten notes on graph paper. Typography-driven hierarchy. The content IS the interface.

Anti-references: any "modern SaaS dashboard" look. No rounded card shadows, no gradient banners, no emoji-heavy headers, no blue primary buttons.

References: printed matter, newspaper ledgers, form documents, field notes, brutalist web design (e.g., Craigslist, HackerNews, gov.uk).

### Design Principles
1. Content IS the interface. No decoration that doesn't carry information.
2. Friction is intentional. The discomfort of seeing disagreement is the feature.
3. Typography hierarchy over color hierarchy. Weight and size, not colored boxes.
4. The tool should feel used, not designed. A worn notebook, not an app store screenshot.
5. Ukrainian language deserves beautiful Cyrillic typography, not Latin-first afterthoughts.
